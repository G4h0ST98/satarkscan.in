The Screen Share Scam

⚠ How the scam works

📡

🏦

Session visible to scammer
⚠ Scammer watching your screen live

Once you share your screen, everything on your phone — OTPs, PINs, balances — is visible to the scammer.

The real scenario

You get a call from someone claiming to be from SBI customer care. Your card was "blocked due to suspicious activity." They sound professional. They have your name. They say to fix it, they need to "verify your device remotely." They ask you to install AnyDesk or TeamViewer from the Play Store — "it's a standard bank procedure."

You install it. You share the 9-digit code. In the next 10 minutes, the scammer opens your banking app on your screen, initiates a transfer, reads your OTP as it arrives on your own phone, and confirms it. You watch helplessly as ₹80,000 leaves your account.

Why this scam works so well

Remote access apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, and QuickSupport are real, legitimate software used by IT support teams globally. This gives the scam instant credibility. The scammer doesn't need your password or OTP directly — they just watch your screen and read it themselves as it appears.

The social engineering is also precise. They call during working hours. They have basic details about you (name, city, sometimes even partial account numbers) purchased from data brokers or leaked databases. They create a sense of emergency — "your account will be permanently blocked in 2 hours."

🛑

No real bank will ever ask you to install any app.

Banks call you to verify identity. They never need to see your screen.

🚩 Red flags to watch for

🚩
The caller asks you to download any app — AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport, mAssist, or anything else — as part of "bank verification."
🚩
They ask for the "session code" or "9-digit ID" shown in the app. This is the key that gives them full control of your phone.
🚩
They stay on call with you throughout, guiding each step. Real bank support ends the call after basic verification.
🚩
The call is from a mobile number (9xxxxxxxx), not a bank's official toll-free number (1800-xxx-xxxx).
🚩
They ask you to keep the session "private" and not tell family members. Isolation is a core part of every social engineering scam.

✅ How to stay safe

✅
Hang up immediately if anyone asks you to install a remote access app. Call your bank back on the number printed on your debit card.
✅
Never share a session code from AnyDesk or TeamViewer with anyone who called you. These codes hand over your entire phone.
✅
Cover your screen when entering PINs or reading OTPs — even on video calls with family. Habit-build this reflex.
✅
Check caller ID: Save your bank's official helpline number. Any incoming call from a different number claiming to be the bank is suspicious by default.
