Banking Scam Alert

The Fake KYC Update Scam

📖 5 min read 🛡 SatarkScan Awareness
⚠ A message you might receive
🏦
SBI-ALERTState Bank of India · Official
Dear Customer, your KYC is PENDING. Your account will be BLOCKED within 24 hours if KYC is not updated. Click to complete: bit.ly/sbi-kyc-update-urgent
⚠ THIS IS A SCAM

Your bank will never threaten to block your account by SMS

The KYC scam is one of the most effective in India because it weaponises fear. The word "blocked" is enough to make most people act without thinking. Scammers know this, and they exploit it relentlessly.

These messages impersonate the RBI, NPCI, SBI, HDFC, Jio, Airtel, and other trusted institutions. The sender ID can look official — SMS spoofing allows scammers to display names like "SBI-ALERT" or "JIO-KYC." But behind it is a fraudster who wants either your UPI PIN or a payment from you.

Ramesh from Hyderabad received an SMS that looked like it was from his bank. The link took him to a convincing fake website that asked for his UPI ID, linked mobile number, and "a verification payment of ₹1" to confirm his identity. He entered his details. Within minutes, ₹47,000 was drained from his account.

Two ways this scam steals from you

🎣
Phishing link. The SMS contains a shortened URL that takes you to a fake bank or telecom website. It looks real. It asks for your UPI PIN, OTP, or Aadhaar number. Never enter these anywhere except your official banking app.
📲
UPI collect request. After you click the link, you are redirected to a page that says "pay ₹1 to verify your identity." This is a UPI collect request. Approving it gives the scammer access to your linked bank account — and they will immediately transfer the maximum possible amount.
🏦

Your real bank will never ask you to verify KYC by paying money.

Verification is free. Always. No exceptions.

Why the "₹1 verification" trick is so dangerous

Many victims think, "It's only ₹1, what can go wrong?" The ₹1 is not the theft — it is the authentication. When you approve a UPI collect request, you are authorising a transaction. The scammer can change the amount on their end or immediately send a second collect request for a much larger sum, exploiting the window of trust.

SatarkScan will flag collect requests, pre-filled amounts, and VPAs with keywords like kyc, verify, and blocked. If you receive a QR code claiming to be for KYC verification, scan it with SatarkScan first.

Red flags

🚩
Any message threatening account suspension or blocking within hours.
🚩
A link to complete KYC — your bank's app handles this, not a link.
🚩
Being asked to pay even ₹1 to "verify" your identity.
🚩
The message asks for your UPI PIN, OTP, or Aadhaar number.

What to do

✅
Open your bank's official app directly. If KYC was genuinely pending, you would see a notice there — not in an SMS.
✅
Never click links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming to be from your bank.
✅
If you receive a QR code for "KYC verification," scan it with SatarkScan before doing anything.
✅
Report fraudulent messages to your telecom via the Sanchar Saathi portal or call 1930.

Your parents or grandparents are the most targeted. Share this with them.
