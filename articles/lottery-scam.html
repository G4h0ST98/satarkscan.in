Prize Fraud Alert

The Lottery & Prize Scam

📖 4 min read 🛡 SatarkScan Awareness
⚠ A message you might receive
KBC Lottery · WhatsApp

🎉 Congratulations! Your mobile number has been selected in theKBC Lucky Draw — ₹25 Lakh!To claim your prize, a small processing fee of ₹1,999 is required.Scan QR below to pay & receive your winnings →

10:34 AM ✓✓
⚠ THIS IS A SCAM

You did not win. Nobody did.

Every year, millions of Indians receive messages claiming they have won a lottery, lucky draw, KBC prize, or government cash reward. The message looks official. The amount sounds life-changing. And there is always one catch — a small "processing fee" that must be paid first via UPI.

There is no prize. There is no lottery. The moment you pay that fee, it is gone. And the scammer moves on to the next victim.

Priya from Pune received a WhatsApp message saying her number had won ₹50 lakh in the "Jio Lucky Draw." She was asked to pay ₹2,500 as a "GST processing charge" via a QR code. She paid. Then another message arrived — she needed to pay ₹5,000 more to "unlock" the prize. She paid again. The prize never came. The number stopped responding.

How the scam is structured

This scam follows a predictable playbook. Understanding it is your best defence:

📱
Step 1 — The hook. A WhatsApp, SMS, or Instagram message announces you have won a large cash prize. It may use the name of a real company — Jio, KBC, Amazon, a government scheme.
💸
Step 2 — The fee. You are told a small "processing fee," "GST charge," or "tax deposit" must be paid first to release your winnings. A QR code is shared.
🔄
Step 3 — The drip. After you pay, another fee appears. Then another. Each payment "unlocks" the next stage. The prize never arrives.
🚪
Step 4 — The vanish. Once you stop paying or push back, the number blocks you and disappears.
🏆

No legitimate lottery or prize scheme ever asks you to pay money to receive money.

If you have to pay, you have already lost.

Why SatarkScan can catch some of these

When a scammer sends you a QR code for the "processing fee," SatarkScan will often flag it. The QR typically has a pre-filled amount (the "processing fee"), may use a VPA containing words like prize, claim, or reward, and often uses a collect request rather than a standard pay link. These all trigger signals in our fraud engine.

However, if the QR code looks completely clean — a personal VPA with no amount pre-filled — our engine may score it LOW RISK. That is a reminder that the scam itself lives in the WhatsApp message, not always in the QR code.

Red flags to watch for

🚩
You were told you won something you never entered.
🚩
Any prize that requires an upfront payment to claim.
🚩
The message creates urgency — "claim within 24 hours or forfeit."
🚩
The sender's number is a regular mobile number, not an official shortcode.
🚩
You are asked to keep the prize secret "for tax reasons."

What to do instead

✅
Delete the message immediately and block the number.
✅
Report it on the National Cybercrime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.
✅
Share this article with family members who might be more vulnerable.
✅
Before scanning any QR related to a prize or reward, run it through SatarkScan first.
