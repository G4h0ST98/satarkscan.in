Action Guide

How to Report UPI Fraud in India

📖 6 min read 🛡 SatarkScan Awareness
⏱ Your action timeline after a UPI scam
1
Within 1 hour
Call your bank — request a freeze
Call the helpline on the back of your debit card. Ask them to freeze the transaction or raise a fraud dispute. Speed matters — money can still be in transit.
2
Within 24 hours
File on Cybercrime portal — 1930
Call the national cybercrime helpline 1930 or file at cybercrime.gov.in. This creates an official record and can trigger a hold on the fraudster's account.
3
Within 3 days
Raise a complaint with your UPI app
Use the dispute/complaint section inside PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm. Attach your cybercrime complaint number to strengthen the case.
4
Within 7 days
File an NPCI complaint if unresolved
If your bank or UPI app has not responded, escalate to NPCI at npci.org.in/contact. NPCI regulates all UPI apps and can intervene.

Why the first hour is critical

When you are scammed on UPI, the money does not vanish immediately. It moves to a "mule account" — an account controlled by the scammer, often someone hired to receive and forward funds. If you report fast enough, banks can place a technical hold on that account before the money is withdrawn or forwarded further. Once it moves again, recovery becomes nearly impossible.

National Cybercrime Helpline: 1930

Available 24×7 · Free to call · Works from any phone in India

All reporting channels at a glance

Channel Where Best for
Helpline 1930 Call from any mobile Immediate freeze request, first report
cybercrime.gov.in Website / portal Official written complaint with evidence
Your bank helpline Number on debit card back Transaction dispute and account freeze
UPI app support Inside PhonePe / GPay / Paytm Dispute the specific transaction
NPCI complaint npci.org.in/contact Escalation if bank does not resolve in 7 days
Local police / FIR Nearest cyber crime cell Required for large amounts, legal proceedings

What to collect before you report

📋
Transaction ID (UTR number) — visible in your UPI app transaction history and the debit SMS from your bank.
📋
Scammer's VPA / UPI ID — the address you sent money to (e.g. scammer@okhdfc). Screenshot it from the transaction details.
📋
Scammer's phone number — if they called you, save the number before they block you. It is evidence.
📋
Screenshots of all conversations — WhatsApp chats, SMS, any messages. Do not delete anything.
📋
Exact time and date — from your bank SMS. This helps the bank locate the transaction quickly in their system.

🚩 Common mistakes that hurt recovery

🚩
Waiting more than 24 hours before reporting. Most recovered funds come from reports made within the first 2–3 hours.
🚩
Deleting chats or call logs "to move on." These are evidence. Keep everything until the case closes.
🚩
Calling a "recovery agent" who promises to get your money back for a fee. This is a second scam targeting people who were already defrauded.
🚩
Only filing with one channel. File everywhere — bank, 1930, cybercrime portal, UPI app. More records mean more pressure.
